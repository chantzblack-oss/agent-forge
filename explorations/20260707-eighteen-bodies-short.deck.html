The pitch · Georgetown · Jan 27 201504 · 14
A physician — Jim Yong Kim, co-founder of Partners In Health — had an idea. Sell bonds. Pay investors fat interest. If a pandemic hits, redirect their principal to the countries fighting it.
“Insurance for humanity.”
Financed by capital markets, not taxpayers. Impossible to argue with — on paper.
read the fine print→
How the bet worked05 · 14
In a near-zero-rate world, this read to bond desks as exotic yield, not existential threat. Coupons flowed no matter what. Principal moved only if the trigger fired.
the rarer the payout, the better the trade→
The fine print06 · 14
To pay, an outbreak had to clear death counts, case counts, border spread — and one more thing:
still speeding up.
At the moment they checked, growth had to be mathematically accelerating. Bend the curve — contain it — and the payout vanished. It measured suffering that hadn’t peaked yet.
