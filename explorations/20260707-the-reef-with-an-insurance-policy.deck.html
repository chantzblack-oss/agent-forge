The Clock You Can’t See12 · 14
The reef has an infection clock.
Most storm fragments don’t seed new colonies — they die, buried and abraded. After Hurricane Earl (2010), broken fragments broke out in lesions “most similar to” white plague, sickening together in the silt.
“Most similar to” — named, never confirmed. The brigades aren’t just outrunning exposure; they’re outrunning infection.

A Frame — Mine, Not Theirs13 · 14
A Geiger counter, where the reef needs a dosimeter.
Wind is an event — a line crossed in an afternoon, paid before the rain stops. Heat is a dose — piling up over weeks (“degree heating weeks”), no click, no alarm. A 2025 Maldives pilot is trying to trigger on heat instead.
Brilliant at spikes. Blind to slopes.
A framing, not a fact.
