# Explore Mode — openers for when you have nothing specific

Copy-paste any of these into a Claude session (or the Telegram bot). They all
work with zero context. The point: **you never need to arrive with a question —
arrive curious and let the session generate the menu.**

## When you're totally blank

- **Surprise me.** "Give me 5 rabbit holes worth an hour of my attention — mix
  science, history, money, and one wildcard. One line each. I'll pick one and
  you go deep."
- **The menu.** "Pitch me 6 explorations like a Netflix menu: a mystery, a
  'how X actually works,' a big idea, a skill, a controversy, and something
  timely from this week's news. I choose, you deliver."
- **Teach me something.** "Teach me one genuinely surprising thing in 3
  paragraphs, then offer 3 directions to go deeper."

## When you want it tuned to you

- **Interview first.** "Ask me 5 quick questions about what I'm into lately,
  then propose 5 explorations matched to my answers."
- **Thread from last time.** "Here's what I explored recently: <topic>. What
  are the 3 most interesting adjacent territories I haven't touched?"

## When you want the heavy machinery

- **Deep dive (research-grade):** "/deep-research <the option you picked>" —
  multi-agent, source-verified, cited report.
- **Cross-model salon:** "Run agent-forge Cross-Model Deliberation on:
  <the option you picked>" — Claude + Gemini argue it out. (Needs
  GEMINI_API_KEY set as an environment secret.)
- **Learning Lab:** "Run agent-forge's Learning Lab / Polymath team on:
  <topic>" — the multi-agent teaching format from your past sessions.

## Standing quality rules (append to anything)

- "…and tell me what I'm wrong about."
- "…give me a recommendation AND what would change your mind."
- "…argue against your own answer before concluding."
